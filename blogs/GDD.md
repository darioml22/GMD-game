# Game Design Document
## This document offers an overview of the ideas, concepts and inspirations behind Wind of Change. 

**Name**: Wind of Change takes its name from the Scorpions’ song with the same title. It was chosen as a representation of the historical time the game is based on. 

**Concept**: In Wind of Change you will be part of the 89 revolution in Romania, that led to the fall of the local dictator and pushed for the fall of the Iron Curtain. You will have to defeat loyalists in order to give the power back to the people.

**Genre**: The game will be a pseudolinear FPS, influenced by early FPS games like Wolfenstein or DOOM, as well as more modern ones like Bioshock or Half-life

**Target Audience**: This game aims to people who enjoy FPS in historical environments. People who enjoy games like the FPS mentioned before or games like Hearts of Iron because of their historical setting would probably enjoy playing Wind of Change.

**Selling Point**: The main difference between Wind of Change and similar games is the historical setting. Not a lot of story centered games cover the east side of the iron curtain, and when they do is mostly during the cold war (Call of Duty: Black Ops Cold War) or in post-apocalyptic worlds (Atomic Heart, S.T.A.L.K.E.R, Metro), and almost all of them are based in Russia or the Soviet Union (with small exceptions like Workers and Resources: Soviet Republic or Papers Please). In contrast to this, Wind of Change is set in the last days of communism in Romania, giving a fresh approach to this genre.

**Player Experience and POV**: The player takes the role of someone who joins the revolution against the current regime. The player will fight alongside friends and neighbours to free the city of Bucharest and defeat Ceaușescu, the country’s dictator. 
This real news footage from ITN was highly used as inspiration and reference for the setting of the game, and will give the reader an idea of the concept behind Wind Of Change

**Visual and Audio Style**: Graphically, the game will be low polly, due to a lack of resources but also to enhance the narrative part of the game. Low polly graphics are, in some way, the equivalent of filming with a 90s camera. Visually, the game takes inspiration in games like Resident Evil 1 or Misery. When it comes to the audio, it will also support this idea, with music from the time, and realistic (as possible) fx sounds.

**Objectives and Progression**: The game will be mostly linear, with one single path the player can go

**Game Systems**: This game will mostly include systems that are standard to this kind of games, such as health, shooting or moving.


The milestones considered for this project are:
Creating a basic world with a character that can move 
Implementing different FPS systems such as health, shooting and menus
Adding enemy AI
