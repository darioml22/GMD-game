# 1st Dev Update
## Milestone: Creating a basic world with a character that can move
### What was expected to be done?
As the first milestone of the development of Wind Of Change, a basic map, together with character movement was set. Originally, the map was planned to be divided into 3 different sections, featuring different environments and enemies. This being:
Streets of Bucharest: The player fights along the streets of the capital city, ensuring the control of the Bucharest radio station as their primary objective.
Communist Party Headquarters: The player continues the adventure defeating all enemies inside the party HQ. The main gameplay difference is that the setup moves from a more “open field” map to a close quarter combat one.
Dictator 's castle. In order to finish the game, the player must defeat the final boss in his castle, which takes inspiration on the Corvin Castle in Romania
The player should be able to move, look around, sprint and jump, in order to make it possible to navigate through the map.

### What was actually done?
The first map was done, using some soviet building assets, and terrain tools. One of the major problems faced was shared issues. At the beginning, the faces of the buildings that were not directly hit by the sun would be completely black. After some research, and teacher feedback, I figured out that I was missing environmental lights, so once I added them the problem was fixed. The other two maps could not be done due to lack of time, so the scope of the project, in map size terms, was reduced to just one map, with the hope that in the future other levels could be added.

For the movement, a FPS controller asset was used, and configured to fit into the game. The speed was changed, the stamina system was removed, and the jump was shortened. At the beginning, the movement of the character was a bit unrealistic (close to games like Superhot), so I had to change for it to look a bit more realistic.

### What is next?
The next milestone for the project is “Implementing different FPS systems such as health, shooting and menus”, which will probably be the longest and hardest part of the whole process, since it consists of a lot of different tasks that will basically build the core of the game.
